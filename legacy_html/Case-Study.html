The Challenge

Twice stalled, and burning a quarter each time.

The client had everything except a path. Two earlier attempts - one with a global consultancy, one with a local fixer - had produced a structure they couldn't operate under and a distributor relationship that went nowhere.

By the time they reached us, the headquarters in Shenzhen had lost confidence that Indonesia was workable at all. The product was proven elsewhere in Southeast Asia. The capital was committed. What was missing was a sequence that respected how Indonesian licensing actually gates a payments business - and a team on the ground who had done it before.

Our Approach

Re-sequence first, build second.

1

Honest diagnostic

Four weeks pulling apart the two failed attempts to find the real blocker - it was ordering, not strategy.

2

License pathway

Pinned the exact activity and KBLI, confirmed ownership headroom, and sequenced permits so none waited on another.

3

Right structure

Designed the PT PMA to the licensing reality - not the other way round - and unwound the unusable prior entity.

4

Local team

Hired and onboarded 14 people for the market, with a country lead who could carry it without us.

The Outcome

Live, legal, and locally run.

Six months after the first call, the company was operating under a correctly-scoped license, with a local team running day-to-day and a country lead reporting cleanly into Shenzhen. The structure that had taken two prior attempts to get wrong took one focused engagement to get right.

More importantly, the headquarters got its confidence back. Indonesia went from "the market we couldn't crack" to a live operation with a credible growth plan - and a board that could finally see the path.
